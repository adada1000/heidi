Writers Blocked

Even the best writers have an awful time reaching a large audience and getting financed. Today, the only way to do so is to hire an agent and find a publisher willing to invest money and bet on the success of the book. The few lucky ones who actually manage to do so are required to give away 90% of their royalties and most of their IP rights.

Heidi disrupts the publishing industry by disintermediating both the authors’ source of funding and their outreach to their reader base.

What it is

Authors can upload their finished and unfinished works onto the platform. Users can read these for free and provide feedback. If a story is gaining momentum, the author is given the opportunity to open a crowdfunding campaign.

How it works

Through an ETH smart contract, a book’s royalties are tokenized and sold for Dai. When a campaign ends, the book can be purchased for a few dollars. Such payments are automatically processed by the smart contract and distributed to the token holders.

Why it's awesome

The blockchain technology allows the intellectual property and royalty rights attached to a book to be fully traceable, assignable and enforceable. It enables micropayments to be immediately and automatically redistributed to investors and royalty tokens to be seamlessly traded on the secondary market.

Long story short...

By bringing technological innovation to a centuries-old industry, Heidi allows everyone to get what they deserve.
Writers are fairly paid for their work.
Investors are rewarded for the risk they take.
Readers can enjoy a good read without being indirectly charged by the publishing industry.




Our strategy


  • Tens of thousands of public domain books will be made available for free on our platform in order to quickly reach a critical mass. Everyone will be able to publish their own books for free or for a fee.

  • We will first focus on the French-speaking market where no serious ebook platform already exists and where free public domain ebooks are not easily available. This market represents around 20% of the world ebook market. We will then expand our operations to other markets once we will be well established.

  • Our knowledge of this market will allow us to easily get in touch with communities of writers looking for an efficient way of getting financed.



Our competitors


  • As opposed to companies such as Amazon, Wattpad or Publica, Heidi flips the subscription model by allowing users to support their author’s works by partaking in the value of the work itself.

  • Amazon takes a large commission, does not provide authors with initial funding and thus does not help them to get the means to promote their book before it's on sale.

  • Wattpad does not allow authors to sell their books on their platform. They only offer to the most successful ones the opportunity to become their sales agent and look for a traditional publisher.

  • Publica offers authors the possibility to realise a "book ICO". Buyers can buy the book half price before its publication and can only read the book in their wallet. Publica does not offer readers any real incentive to use their platform.
